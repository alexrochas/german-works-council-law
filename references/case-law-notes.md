# Case Law Notes

This file is a lean digest of recurring BAG themes that often matter in works-council practice. It is not exhaustive and it is not a substitute for checking the full decision text.

## How To Use This File

- Start with the statute text first.
- Use these notes to flag where BAG case law sharpens or limits the statutory reading.
- Cite docket number, decision date, and the official BAG URL when a point materially depends on precedent.
- If the issue is litigation-sensitive or the facts are close to the line, verify the full decision text before making a confident recommendation.
- Treat every note here as informational only; it does not replace German-qualified legal advice.

## Monitoring And Technical Systems

### BAG, Beschluss vom 11.12.2018 - 1 ABR 13/17

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-13-17/
- Core point:
  For `§ 87 Abs. 1 Nr. 6 BetrVG`, a technical system counts as a monitoring device when it is objectively capable of collecting and recording behavior or performance information. A subjective wish to monitor is not required.
- Practical use:
  Helpful for survey tools, feedback platforms, HR systems, ticketing tools, analytics, and similar systems that can produce employee-related performance or conduct signals.
- Caution:
  This does not mean every change to content or questionnaires automatically triggers fresh co-determination; the concrete system change still matters.

### BAG, Beschluss vom 11.12.2012 - 1 ABR 78/11

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-78-11/
- Core point:
  Video surveillance of operational premises falls within `§ 87 Abs. 1 Nr. 6 BetrVG`, but the scope of co-determination can be narrowed where binding legal or administrative requirements leave the employer no design discretion.
- Practical use:
  Useful when the question is not only whether co-determination exists, but how much room remains for bargaining over design, retention, coverage, or operation.

### BAG, Beschluss vom 13.12.2016 - 1 ABR 7/15

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-7-15/
- Core point:
  A Facebook page function that allows users to post comments about employee behavior or performance can be a technical monitoring device under `§ 87 Abs. 1 Nr. 6 BetrVG`.
- Practical use:
  Useful for public platforms, feedback features, marketplaces, app-store reviews, or any external channel that can systematically generate employee-related performance information.
- Caution:
  The case is strongest where the feature enables structured or persistent observation of employee conduct, not merely generic employer marketing.

## Employee Monitoring, Evidence, And Privacy Limits

### BAG, Urteil vom 27.07.2017 - 2 AZR 681/16

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/2-azr-681-16/
- Core point:
  Use of a software keylogger is not justified under employee-data rules without a concrete, fact-based suspicion of a criminal offense or similarly serious breach.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when assessing whether deeply intrusive monitoring is lawful and whether resulting evidence is likely to be usable.
- Privacy overlap:
  Read alongside `§ 26 BDSG`, `Art. 5 GDPR`, `Art. 6 GDPR`, and `Art. 32 GDPR`.

## Hiring, Integration, And Consent Refusal

### BAG, Beschluss vom 12.06.2019 - 1 ABR 5/18

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-5-18/
- Core point:
  A person can be "eingestellt" under `§ 99 BetrVG` when they are integrated into the establishment to pursue its operational purpose through instruction-bound activity. A refusal under `§ 99 Abs. 2 Nr. 1 BetrVG` requires a norm whose purpose is to prevent the measure itself.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when evaluating whether a particular assignment triggers `§ 99`, and whether a cited legal or internal-rule breach is strong enough to support refusal.
- Extra note:
  The decision also reinforces that there is no general vacancy-posting duty under `§ 93 BetrVG` unless the works council has demanded posting or a binding agreement requires it.

## Dismissal Hearing Procedure

### BAG, Urteil vom 25.05.2016 - 2 AZR 345/15

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/2-azr-345-15/
- Core point:
  A works council statement shortens the `§ 102 BetrVG` waiting period only if the employer can unmistakably see that it is final. If that is unclear and the employer wants to dismiss early, the employer should obtain clarification.
- Practical use:
  Useful in timeline disputes around whether the hearing period was really complete before notice was issued.

## Operational Change Thresholds

### BAG, Beschluss vom 22.03.2016 - 1 ABR 12/14

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-12-14/
- Core point:
  A "grundlegende Änderung" under `§ 111 Satz 3 Nr. 4 BetrVG` requires substantial, significant effects on operations, work methods, or working conditions. Not every process or management initiative reaches the threshold of a Betriebsänderung.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when screening reorganizations, lean-management programs, workflow redesigns, or operational methods to decide whether `§ 111 BetrVG` is likely triggered.

## Election Structure And Validity

### BAG, Beschluss vom 23.11.2016 - 7 ABR 3/15

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/7-abr-3-15/
- Core point:
  The rules on a gemeinsamer Betrieb remain central when assessing whether a single works council was the correct election unit; the statutory presumptions in `§ 1 Abs. 2 BetrVG` are especially important.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when the real fight is whether separate entities operate one establishment or several for election purposes.

### BAG, Beschluss vom 29.03.2017 - 7 ABR 42/15

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/7-abr-42-15/
- Core point:
  Election errors under the WO matter when they could have affected the result; not every procedural mistake saves or destroys an election automatically.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when triaging election-challenge risk and distinguishing merely formal defects from outcome-relevant problems.

## Remedies After A Co-Determination Breach

### BAG, Beschluss vom 23.03.2021 - 1 ABR 31/19

- Official source:
  https://www.bundesarbeitsgericht.de/entscheidung/1-abr-31-19/
- Core point:
  A Betriebsrat's remedial claim after a `§ 87 Abs. 1 BetrVG` breach reaches the end of the unlawful state, but not every downstream consequence must be unwound.
- Practical use:
  Helpful when framing what relief is realistically available after a system or process has already gone live without proper participation.
